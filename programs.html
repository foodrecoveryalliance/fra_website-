Gleaning Program

Rescuing surplus food from farms and businesses before it goes to waste.

Our gleaning program works directly with farms, grocery stores, restaurants, and food distributors to rescue perfectly good food that would otherwise be wasted. Youth volunteers coordinate pickups, sort and prepare food for distribution, and ensure it reaches families in need.

We've built strong relationships with over 20 partner farms and organizations, creating a reliable network that diverts thousands of pounds of food from landfills every month. Each recovery operation is an opportunity for youth to learn about food systems, develop logistics skills, and see the direct impact of their work.

Through this program, we're not just addressing food waste; we're teaching young people about sustainable agriculture, food supply chains, and the importance of resource conservation. Volunteers and leaders gain hands-on experience in operations management, community organizing, and community well-being.

Community Food Outreach Program

Bringing fresh, nutritious food directly to those who need it most.

In our community food outreach program our volunteers collaborate with local farms and organizations to rescue surplus produce and transform it into meaningful community impact. From organizing harvesting and weeding days at partner farms to safely preparing nutritious processed foods for local food shelters, volunteers play an active role in every stage of food recovery. This program not only reduces food waste, but also empowers young people with leadership, teamwork, and real-world skills while supporting food security in their communities.

Who It Serves

  • Families with children experiencing food insecurity
  • Farm owners
  • Individuals in transitional housing
  • Communities with limited food access

Where It Operates

  • Farms across Victoria and Vancoover
  • Community centers and markets
  • Food Recovery Alliance Community Garden

Measurable Outcomes

  • 200+ meals delivered annually
  • 200+ youth volunteers
  • 10+ outreach events hosted

Youth Education Program

Teaching youth about food systems and empowering them to advocate for change.

Our Youth Education Program brings food systems education into schools and community spaces, teaching young people about the connections between food waste, food insecurity, and social justice. We train youth to become advocates for systemic change in their communities.

Through interactive workshops, school presentations, and community events, we help students understand where their food comes from, why food waste matters, and how they can take action. Our curriculum covers topics from sustainable agriculture and nutrition to food policy and environmental justice.

Beyond education, we support youth in developing advocacy campaigns, meeting with local officials, and organizing community events. This program creates a pipeline of informed, engaged young leaders who understand that food recovery is about creating systemic change.

Who It Serves

  • Middle and high school students
  • Youth leadership groups and clubs at schools
  • Community organizations and youth centers
  • Teachers seeking food systems and global health curricula

Where It Operates

  • 3 local schools
  • Summer programs and workshops

Measurable Outcomes

  • 250+ students reached through workshops
  • 15+ youth advocacy leaders
